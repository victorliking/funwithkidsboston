## TD Garden's Official Team Store: The Holy Grail of Kids Celtics Gear

The ProShop at TD Garden remains the ultimate destination for authentic kids Boston Celtics jerseys, and trust me, we've made this pilgrimage more times than I can count. Located right inside the arena at 100 Legends Way, this massive store carries the most comprehensive selection of youth jerseys you'll find anywhere in New England.

What makes this spot special isn't just the selection – though they typically stock sizes from toddler 2T all the way up to youth XL – it's the experience. My kids love trying on different player jerseys, and the staff is incredibly patient with little ones who can't decide between Jaylen Brown or Robert Williams III. The authentic Nike jerseys run about $80-$90 for kids, while the replica versions are more budget-friendly at $55-$65.

Pro tip: Visit during non-game days if possible. We learned this the hard way after waiting 45 minutes in line before a playoff game! The store is typically less crowded on weekday afternoons, and you'll get much better service. Plus, parking is easier – we usually park in the North Station Garage ($25-$35 depending on events) and it's a short walk.

The selection varies by season, but they always maintain strong stock of the current roster's jerseys in kids' sizes. During our last visit in October, they had an entire wall dedicated to youth jerseys, including special edition City jerseys that you simply won't find anywhere else.

## Dick's Sporting Goods and Local Retailers: Budget-Friendly Options

When you're looking for a kids Boston Celtics jersey without breaking the bank, Dick's Sporting Goods locations throughout Greater Boston offer solid alternatives. The Legacy Place location in Dedham (680 Legacy Place) and the Assembly Row spot in Somerville (395 Artisan Way) typically maintain good youth jersey selections.

Dick's often runs seasonal sales where you can snag kids' jerseys for $35-$45, especially during back-to-school promotions in late summer. My middle child got his first Marcus Smart jersey there for just $32 during a Presidents' Day sale, and it's held up beautifully through countless playground games and wash cycles.

The Modell's locations (though fewer now) and local Faneuil Hall shops also carry kids Boston Celtics jerseys, but quality can vary significantly. We've found some great deals at the souvenir shops near Quincy Market – one vendor there regularly stocks youth jerseys for $25-$30, though they're typically screen-printed rather than embroidered.

Getting There: If you're heading to Assembly Row, take the Orange Line to Assembly station – it's literally right there. For Legacy Place, driving is easier; they have plenty of free parking, which is a blessing for us suburban parents!

## Seasonal Shopping Strategy: Timing Your Jersey Purchase

After years of jersey shopping with three kids (and their friends), I've learned that timing is everything when hunting for the perfect kids Boston Celtics jersey. The best deals typically appear during three key windows throughout the year.

Late August through early September offers back-to-school promotions at major retailers. This is when we scored my youngest's first jersey – a size 4T Kemba Walker that he wore constantly (even though Walker was traded shortly after!). Many stores discount previous season jerseys by 40-50% during this period.

Mid-January brings post-holiday clearance sales, perfect for grabbing next year's jersey or treating yourself to that authentic version you've been eyeing. The ProShop at TD Garden often runs "Green Week" promotions in late January, offering 20-30% off kids' merchandise.

End of season (April/May) presents the deepest discounts, though selection can be limited. We've found incredible deals on kids Boston Celtics jerseys during playoff runs when stores are trying to clear inventory. Last spring, my neighbor snagged three youth jerseys for $25 each at a Somerville sporting goods store.

Heads up: Avoid shopping right before major games or during playoff runs unless you're willing to pay premium prices. We learned this lesson during the 2022 Finals when jersey prices seemingly doubled overnight!

## Online Shopping vs. In-Person: Pros and Cons for Boston Parents

Living in Greater Boston gives us the luxury of choice between online shopping and visiting physical stores for kids Boston Celtics jerseys. Both have their merits, and we've utilized both approaches extensively.

Online shopping through the official NBA Store or Celtics website offers the broadest selection and often features exclusive youth designs. The customization options are fantastic – my daughter loves her personalized "BOSTON KID" jersey that we ordered online. Prices are typically competitive, and shipping to the Boston area usually takes 3-5 business days.

However, sizing can be tricky online, especially for rapidly growing kids. We've had to return two jerseys that arrived too small, despite following the size charts carefully. The return process was smooth, but it delayed my son getting his jersey for the start of the season.

In-person shopping lets you check quality, try on sizes, and avoid shipping delays. Plus, there's something special about the experience – my kids still talk about the day we bought their first "real" Celtics jerseys at TD Garden. The immediate gratification is worth the extra effort, especially when your seven-year-old is bouncing off the walls with excitement.

Local stores also offer unique finds. We discovered vintage-style kids Boston Celtics jerseys at a Cambridge sporting goods shop that weren't available online. The owner, a lifelong Celtics fan himself, gave us great advice about sizing and care instructions.

## Caring for Your Kids Boston Celtics Jersey: A Parent's Guide

Once you've invested in that perfect kids Boston Celtics jersey, proper care becomes essential – trust me, I've learned this through trial and plenty of errors! After washing countless jerseys over the years, I've developed a system that keeps them looking great season after season.

Always wash kids' jerseys inside-out in cold water. The graphics and numbers are more delicate than they appear, and hot water will cause them to crack or fade. We use a gentle detergent and avoid bleach completely. My biggest mistake early on was throwing my oldest's Paul Pierce jersey in with a regular hot water load – the number started peeling within three washes.

Air drying is crucial, even though it's tempting to toss them in the dryer with everything else. We hang jerseys on our basement drying rack, away from direct sunlight which can fade the green. In our humid New England summers, we run a dehumidifier to speed the process.

For game day stains (and there will be many!), treat spots immediately. Ice cream, hot dog mustard, and playground dirt are common culprits in our house. A gentle stain remover applied before washing usually does the trick. For tough stains, we've had success with a paste made from baking soda and water.

Storage matters too, especially if you're keeping jerseys as kids grow. We use cedar hangers and garment bags for long-term storage, which has kept our collection of outgrown jerseys in great condition for younger siblings or friends.

## FAQ

### Where can I find the cheapest kids Boston Celtics jersey in Boston?

The most affordable options are typically found at discount sporting goods stores in neighborhoods like Dorchester or Chelsea, where we've seen kids' jerseys for $20-$30. Dick's Sporting Goods during major sales events is also excellent – we've scored authentic youth jerseys for under $40. End-of-season clearances at the TD Garden ProShop can offer surprising deals, though selection is limited. Avoid tourist areas like Faneuil Hall for bargains; prices there are inflated for visitors.

### What's the best size to buy for a growing child?

Based on our experience with three kids, buy slightly larger rather than smaller. Kids Boston Celtics jerseys in youth sizes tend to run true to size, but children grow quickly. My rule of thumb: if your child is between sizes, go up one. The loose fit actually looks more authentic and allows for comfortable movement during play. However, don't go more than one size up – oversized jerseys can look sloppy and may not be as comfortable.

### Are there any local Boston stores that offer jersey customization?

Yes! The ProShop at TD Garden offers full customization services, including adding your child's name and favorite number to any kids Boston Celtics jersey. The process takes about 45 minutes and costs an additional $25-$30. Some Dick's Sporting Goods locations also provide customization services. We've also found excellent custom work at Twins Enterprises in the North End (they do a lot of team jerseys for local youth leagues) and at several shops around Fenway that specialize in sports apparel.

### When is the best time to buy kids Celtics jerseys during the season?

Early November through mid-December offers the best selection and reasonable prices before holiday demand spikes. Avoid shopping during playoff runs when prices increase significantly. Post-All-Star break (February/March) can offer good deals as retailers prepare for spring inventory. If you're not picky about having the latest player, end-of-season sales in May provide excellent bargains on kids Boston Celtics jerseys, perfect for buying ahead for next season.

### Can I find vintage or throwback kids Boston Celtics jerseys locally?

Absolutely! Check out Bobby from Boston in the Back Bay (19 Thayer Street) – they occasionally have youth vintage jerseys. Antiques at 80 Charles in Beacon Hill sometimes stocks sports memorabilia including kids' gear. We found an amazing Larry Bird youth jersey at a Somerville consignment shop last year. Online marketplaces like Facebook Marketplace often have local sellers offering gently used kids Boston Celtics jerseys, but always inspect carefully for authenticity and condition.
